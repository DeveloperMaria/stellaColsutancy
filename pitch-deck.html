Contents of a Pitch Deck

A description of the problem or gap in the market that your company is addressing

A clear explanation of how your product or service solves the problem, and the benefits it brings to the customer

A clear and compelling explanation of the unique benefits and solutions that your business offers to its customers

 A description of your company's products or services, including how they meet customer needs, pricing, and unique features or benefits

An analysis of your target market, including size, growth prospects, demographics, and buying habits

A clear explanation of how the business makes money and how it will scale.

Provide evidence of traction, such as customer testimonials, case studies or metrics that demonstrate the effectiveness of your product or service

An overview of your competitors, their strengths and weaknesses, and how your company differentiates itself from the competition

Financial projections, such as income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements, that demonstrate your company's ability to generate revenue and become profitable.

Clearly outline how the funds will be used and the expected returns on investment

Introduce the key members of your team and their qualifications

What you want the investors to do next

It's worth noting that the contents of the pitch deck can vary depending on the industry, the size of the round, the stage of the company, and the target audience, but the above are the most common contents you will find in a pitch deck.
